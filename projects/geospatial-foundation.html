Overview

The 7-Day Wait

The challenge was existential: when Sylhet floods during monsoon, emergency coordinators need maps within hours to route aid, not seven days later when manual GIS analysts finish tracing water extent.

We built HAWKEYE, a vision transformer pipeline (SegFormer-B2-lite, 24M parameters) that achieves 91% mean intersection-over-union on flood segmentation through physics-informed pseudo-labeling.

The technique bypasses the label bottleneck entirely. We generate training masks automatically by fusing three physical rules: (1) Sentinel-1 SAR VV polarization backscatter <0.2 dB identifies smooth water surfaces, (2) Normalized Difference Water Index (NDWI) >0.1 from Sentinel-2 isolates high-moisture pixels, (3) SRTM digital elevation model slope <0.05 radians enforces gravitational constraint (water accumulates in depressions, not hillsides). Pixels satisfying all three rules become positive training examples.

This physics-generated dataset trains SegFormer-B2 in 15 epochs on a single V100 GPU (6 hours, $4 compute cost). The trained model processes a 2,847 km² Sentinel-1 scene in 8 minutes on CPU, outputs probability masks, and exports GeoJSON polygons—total pipeline runtime 30 minutes.

Role

Data Engineering (Copernicus API integration, GDAL raster processing), Model Architecture (SegFormer implementation, PINN loss functions), Training (hyperparameter tuning, early stopping), Validation (confusion matrices, spatial error analysis), Deployment (FastAPI serving, GeoServer integration), Documentation (6-panel explainer visualizations, confidence heatmaps).

Outcome

99.7% reduction in processing time. A single fine-tuned foundation model (in development) will replace 15 separate task-specific models, enabling multi-task transfer learning. Government partnership discussions with SPARRSO (Bangladesh Space Research) and Department of Disaster Management progressing toward $500K-2M annual service contracts.
